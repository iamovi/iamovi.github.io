Dear Brain,

An open letter to the part of me that's apparently nocturnal, chaotic, and deeply committed to my suffering.

We need to talk. Seriously. Not at 2 AM — now, in broad daylight, where you can't hide behind the dark and pretend this is "reflection time." Because 2 AM is not a deep reflection session. It's full-on sabotage. And yes, I see what you're doing.

02:00 AM

Why are you showing me that embarrassing text from 2014? Why are you questioning all my life choices? And why — oh why — are you suddenly convinced that the ending of that movie I watched six months ago is secretly about me?

Spoiler: it's not.

You're supposed to help me sleep. Instead, you're hosting a late-night TED Talk titled Everything You've Done Wrong and Why You'll Fail Forever. Thanks a lot.

The Random Thoughts You Choose to Obsess Over

Who taught you that obsessing over minutiae is productive? No one. Yet here we are:

  • Dissecting that one comment I made at a work meeting in 2018 — yeah, the one I had specifically blocked out
  • Debating whether I should've chosen almond milk instead of oat milk last week
  • Imagining conversations with my high school crush that will never happen

Meanwhile, sleep? Forget it. You're busy keeping me awake. Congratulations.

"Why did I say 'you too' when the waiter told me to enjoy my meal?" — me, apparently, at 2:17 AM, unprompted.

The Creativity Trap

02:15 AM

And don't get me started on when you pretend to be helpful. Suddenly, 2:15 AM is prime brainstorming hour. You convince me I need a life plan, a startup idea, and a novel manuscript — all before sunrise.

Brain, do you know what people call this? Insomnia. Some also call it torture. But sure, let's call it creativity. Let's call it hustle. Let's call it whatever makes you feel better about robbing me of eight hours.

The worst part? The ideas feel genuinely good in the moment. A new project. A product. A plan so detailed it has subfolders. And then morning comes and I open my notes app to find:

"what if social media but for dogs who code" — 2:41 AM, last Tuesday.

Incredible. Truly a visionary at work.

The Existential Spiral

02:47 AM

Then you go full philosopher. Suddenly, I'm questioning the meaning of life, my purpose, and whether I should have learned Mandarin instead of Spanish.

Fun fact: I can barely conjugate "to be" in English. Which I feel is relevant.

But no — apparently I need to discover the grand truth of existence before sunrise. Cool. Love it. Very necessary. Definitely not a symptom of something I should talk to someone about.

Here's what the 3 AM philosopher always conveniently forgets: nothing has ever been solved at 3 AM. Not a single war ended. Not one relationship healed. Not one existential crisis resolved. The universe has been around for 13.8 billion years. It will wait until 10 AM.

A Simple Request

So here's the deal, Brain. A formal agreement. Terms and conditions:

  • Stop replaying every social mistake I've ever made — especially the ones from before I had a fully developed prefrontal cortex
  • Stop creating imaginary arguments I'll never have (and somehow losing them)
  • Stop convincing me that my life depends on solving the mysteries of the universe at 2:47 AM
  • Stop generating startup ideas when I'm horizontal
  • Stop. The. Oat. Milk. Debate.

Let's save that existential energy for, say — 10 AM? Maybe after coffee? Maybe after three alarms, two groans, and crying into my pillow? We can question everything then. I'll even make a list.

Closing Thoughts

You're brilliant, I'll give you that. You write code that actually works. You remember things I never deliberately stored. You connected two unrelated ideas once and it led to something genuinely cool. I respect the grind.

But you're also a little sadistic. And while I appreciate your dedication to my growth and reflection — can we maybe take a break? For eight hours? Twelve would be nice. Sixteen if you're feeling generous.

The 2014 text isn't coming back. The meeting comment from 2018 has been forgotten by everyone except you. The oat milk was fine. The movie ending was not about me.

We're going to be okay. But only if you let me sleep.

Sincerely,
Me (who really just wants eight hours)
